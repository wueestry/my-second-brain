# RED FLAGS, LEAKED CHATS, AND A FINAL FAREWELL | SMASHING SECURITY #428

---

![[meta/assets/smashing-security.png|150]]

## Summary:

The episode "Red flags, leaked chats, and a final farewell" from Smashing Security delves into the complex issues surrounding the T dating app’s significant data breaches, highlighting the risks and consequences of legacy data storage vulnerabilities and privacy violations. The hosts discuss the app’s original intent to protect women by sharing warnings about problematic men, but also the controversies and cybersecurity failures that led to the exposure of sensitive user data, including private messages and identity documents. Alongside this, the episode reflects on the evolution of the podcast itself, with co-host Carol Terrio announcing her departure after nearly nine years, marking a poignant moment of change and reflection on the growth and challenges in the tech and cybersecurity landscape.

## Key Insights & Analysis:

### Legacy Data Storage Vulnerabilities and Data Breach Impact

#### Explanation:

The episode reveals how the T dating app suffered a major security breach due to a compromised legacy data storage system, which contained unprotected images and documents from users who signed up before February 2024. This breach exposed highly sensitive data, including selfies, ID documents, and private messages, to malicious actors on platforms like 4chan. The hosts emphasise the dangers of poor data management and outdated security practices, illustrating how legacy systems can become critical weak points in cybersecurity.

### Ethical and Privacy Challenges of "Name and Shame" Apps

#### Explanation:

The discussion highlights the ethical dilemmas posed by apps designed to allow women to share warnings about men’s behaviour, which, while well-intentioned, can lead to privacy violations, false accusations, and identity theft. The hosts explore the tension between protecting vulnerable users and the potential for misuse or harm to innocent individuals, reflecting on the complexity of balancing safety with fairness and privacy.

### Ongoing Security Issues and User Data Exposure

#### Explanation:

Beyond the initial breach, the episode reveals a more recent vulnerability allowing access to a database containing 1.1 million messages, including highly personal and identifiable information. This ongoing exposure highlights the persistent security challenges faced by apps handling sensitive communications and the real-world impact on users’ privacy and safety.

They question whether platforms should suspend apps during security incidents and discuss existing legal mechanisms for individuals to check on potentially dangerous partners, highlighting gaps and challenges in digital and legal protections.
