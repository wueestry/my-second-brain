# ORACLE'S CSO MARY ANN DAVIDSON QUIETLY DEPARTS | RISKY BUSINESS #803

---

![[meta/assets/risky-business.jpg|150]]

> [!summary]
> This podcast episode discusses recent cybersecurity incidents and industry trends, focusing on the challenges of managing complex breaches, regulatory issues, and the evolving tactics of cybercriminals.
> It also explores how organisations are adapting by integrating security services and balancing strong protection with user-friendly authentication.

## Oracle Cloud Security Breach and Leadership Changes (00:30–04:00)

- Oracle experienced a significant security breach in its legacy cloud environment, initially denied and poorly communicated.

- The incident exposed weaknesses in Oracle’s security culture and may have influenced the departure of a long-serving security executive.

- Highlights challenges in managing legacy systems and maintaining transparency in large organisations.

## Financial Fraud via Brokerage Account Exploitation (09:00–12:30)

- A Chinese cybercrime group shifted from targeting bank accounts to exploiting brokerage accounts to manipulate penny stock prices.

- They use compromised accounts to buy stocks at inflated prices, complicating tracing and legal enforcement.

- Demonstrates the industrial scale and adaptability of modern financial cybercrime operations.

## Security Vulnerabilities in Dating Apps and Data Exposure (12:50–15:30)

- The “T on her” app, created as a counterpart to the “Tea” app, was found to have critical security flaws such as unprotected APIs and poor user data validation.

- Illustrates risks of hastily developed applications handling sensitive personal data without adequate security controls.

## UK Government’s Demand for Access to Encrypted Apple Data (15:30–19:00)

- The UK government requested Apple to provide access to encrypted data of American users, leading Apple to disable advanced data protection for UK accounts.

- Highlights tension between government surveillance demands and user privacy protections.

- The outcome remains unclear, with political and jurisdictional complexities involved.

## Workday CRM Account Compromise and Third-Party Security Risks (19:00–21:30)

- Attackers compromised Workday staff accounts in an upstream CRM system to harvest customer contact information.

- Used this data to target customers for fraud and extortion.

- Emphasises vulnerabilities introduced by interconnected enterprise platforms and the need to secure third-party systems.

## Industrial Control Systems Attack by Pro-Russian Hackers (25:30–27:00)

- Norwegian authorities attribute an attack on a dam’s industrial control systems to pro-Russian hackers.

- Attackers attempted to manipulate physical infrastructure controls but caused no significant damage due to robust system design.

- Raises concerns about politically motivated cyberattacks on critical infrastructure.

## Guidance on Operational Technology Asset Inventorying (26:30–28:30)

- CISA and international partners released detailed guidance on cataloguing and managing operational technology (OT) assets.

- Addresses widespread lack of dedicated security teams in critical infrastructure sectors.

- Provides practical examples and data points necessary for effective OT security management.

## Cybersecurity Industry Consolidation: Accenture’s Acquisition of CyberCX (37:00–42:00)

- Accenture acquired Australian cybersecurity firm CyberCX, a successful private equity roll-up combining multiple security disciplines.

- Integrated services include penetration testing, incident response, and managed security services.

- Reflects growing demand for comprehensive, scalable security solutions in complex enterprise environments.

## Balancing Strong Authentication with Usability in Critical Environments (45:30–50:00)

- Development of advanced authentication solutions such as custom credential providers for Windows and integration with Apple’s business frameworks.

- Aims to enhance security while maintaining ease of use.

- Real-world example: the 911 Centre in Washington requires streamlined login processes in life-critical settings.

## Open Source Security Tools and Expert Auditing (51:00–54:00)

- Making security tools source-available encourages community feedback and expert penetration testing.

- Specialists with deep system knowledge help identify vulnerabilities early.

- Builds trust in enterprise-grade software through transparency and rigorous security assessments.

## Implementing Single Logout Standards and Dynamic Security Signals (56:00–57:00)

- Adoption of standards like backchannel logout and SAML single logout synchronises user sessions across multiple applications.

- Enables coordinated logouts to improve security.

- Dynamic signals such as group membership changes or suspicious logins should trigger automated logout requests for real-time responsiveness.
