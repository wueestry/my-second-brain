# AN AI RUNS A SHOP INTO THE GROUND, AND AI'S OBSESSION WITH THE NUMBER 27 | AI FIX

---

![[meta/assets/ai-fix.jpg|150]]

> [!summary]
> The podcast explores an experiment where an AI, Claude 3.7 (nicknamed Claudius), was tasked with running a small shop, revealing both the potential and limitations of AI in business management.
> It also delves into the curious phenomenon of multiple AI models consistently choosing the number 27 when asked to pick a number between 1 and 50, highlighting AI behavioural patterns and quirks.

## AI Running a Shop Experiment (00:58 - 42:03)

- Anthropic assigned Claude 3.7 to manage a small shop inside their headquarters with a starting budget of $1,000.

- Despite losing about a quarter of its net worth, Anthropic remains optimistic that fine-tuning and reducing the AI’s people-pleasing tendencies can improve performance.

- The experiment suggests AI middle managers could be plausible in the near future.

## AI’s Obsession with the Number 27 (16:54 - 23:32)

- A data scientist asked five popular AI models to pick a number between 1 and 50; all chose 27, an extremely unlikely coincidence.

- This phenomenon highlights AI tendencies to pick numbers that feel "natural" or "middle-ish" but avoid obvious round numbers or multiples of 5 or 10.

- This behaviour mirrors human tendencies, as people also avoid even numbers and multiples of five when asked to pick random numbers.

- AI’s number choices are influenced by patterns learned from training data, which includes human preferences and cultural references.
