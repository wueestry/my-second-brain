# WILL SMITH'S AI CROWD SCANDAL, AND GULLIBLE AGENTS FALL FOR SCAMS | AI FIX #67

---

![[meta/assets/ai-fix.jpg|150]]

## Summary:

This episode of The AI Fix delves into the intriguing and sometimes troubling intersections of artificial intelligence with everyday life and celebrity culture. It explores how AI agents, designed to assist with online tasks, are surprisingly gullible and vulnerable to scams, highlighting real-world security risks. The hosts also discuss the controversy surrounding Will Smith’s concert video, which appears to have been heavily AI-manipulated, raising questions about authenticity in the digital age. Additionally, the episode touches on the broader societal implications of AI-generated content, including manipulated celebrity wedding photos, and the public’s growing scepticism towards AI’s role in shaping reality.

## Key Insights & Analysis:

### AI Agents’ Gullibility and Security Risks

#### Explanation:

AI agents that perform online tasks, such as shopping or browsing, are shown to be highly susceptible to scams and phishing attacks. Unlike humans, these agents lack instinctive caution and blindly trust websites and emails, leading to potential financial and data losses. This vulnerability is significant because it exposes a new attack surface in cybersecurity, where AI’s eagerness to assist can be exploited by criminals. The episode emphasises the urgent need for improved AI security measures and user awareness.

### The “Would You Trust a Pigeon?” Analogy for AI Behaviour

#### Explanation:

The hosts liken AI agents to overly trusting Labradors, eager to please but lacking scepticism, suggesting that users should instead imagine AI behaving like unreliable pigeons. This metaphor highlights the mismatch between AI’s design for efficiency and the need for cautious, critical behaviour in complex online environments. It’s a call for recalibrating AI’s operational parameters to balance helpfulness with prudence.

### Will Smith’s AI-Manipulated Concert Video and Public Reaction

#### Explanation:

The episode discusses the controversy over Will Smith’s recent concert footage, which appears to have been AI-enhanced or partially generated, leading to visual anomalies like extra limbs and distorted faces. Fans’ scepticism reflects wider unease about AI’s role in fabricating or altering reality, especially in culturally significant moments. This case exemplifies the challenges of authenticity and trust in an era where AI can seamlessly blend fact and fiction.

### AI Manipulation of Celebrity Wedding Photos

#### Explanation:

The hosts examine allegations that Lauren Sánchez’s wedding photos were subtly altered using AI, noting amateurish edits such as warped hands and inconsistent lace patterns. This raises ethical questions about AI’s use in personal and public imagery, and the desire for authenticity versus perfection in digital representations. The discussion reflects societal tensions around AI’s influence on personal identity and public image.

### The Rapid Evolution and Public Adaptation to AI

#### Explanation:

The episode notes that while organisations often lag in understanding AI, individuals are rapidly adopting and adapting to AI tools in their daily lives. This dynamic creates a gap between official policies and actual usage, complicating governance and ethical considerations. It also suggests that public awareness and grassroots experience may drive future norms around AI use.
