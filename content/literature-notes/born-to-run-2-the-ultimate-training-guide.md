# BORN TO RUN 2: THE ULTIMATE TRAINING GUIDE

---

![[meta/assets/born-to-run-2.jpg|150]]

> [!summary]
> "Born to Run 2: The Ultimate Training Guide" by [[Christopher McDougall]] explores the transformative power of running by combining ancestral wisdom with modern training techniques to help runners of all levels run injury-free, efficiently, and joyfully.
> The book emphasises a holistic approach that integrates form, fitness, nutrition, focus, footwear, fun, and community to unlock the full potential of natural running.

## The Free Seven: A Balanced Approach to Running (pp. 30, 32-34, 54-223)

The book introduces the concept of the Free Seven, a set of interconnected elements essential for effective and enjoyable running. These include:

### Food: Your for is not your coach (pp. 30, 54-63)

- Focus on adopting a diet that stabilises blood sugar and maximises energy by favouring slow-burning fats over processed carbohydrates

  - Supported by the Two-Week Test to assess dietary impact.

### Fitness: Become the master mechanic (pp. 30, 88-95)

- Emphasise strengthening dormant muscles, such as glutes, through simple exercises like Wall Squats and Foot Core routines to correct imbalances and build resilience.

### Form: The art of easy (pp. 33, 106-114)

- Improve running biomechanics with a four-exercise routine, including the Five-Minute Fix, to promote elastic leg recoil and reduce injury risk, rather than relying solely on footwear changes.

### Focus: Faster, farther and forever (pp. 33, 124-137)

- Runners have to master different effort gears (Full Burn, Threshold, and All Day) using heart-rate awareness and pace control to build speed and endurance efficiently.

### Footwear: First, do no harm (pp. 33, 140-163)

- Challenges the conventional shoe industry by promoting minimalist footwear or barefoot running to encourage natural foot mechanics and prevent injury.

### Fun: If it feels like work, you’re working too hard (pp. 34, 164-187)

- Incorporate music, playful drills, and social runs to maintain motivation and enjoyment, highlighting the psychological benefits of running.

### Family: Those who sweat together soar together (pp. 34, 188-223)

- Run as a communal activity, including running with children, dogs, and partners, to foster connection and shared purpose.

## The 90-Day Run Free Programme: Structured Progression for All Runners (pp. 224-237)

This detailed training plan guides runners through a progressive sequence of workouts, combining strength, form drills, and varied intensity runs. Key features include:

### Daily Sequence and Skill Integration (pp. 227-230)

- Perform exercises in a specific order to build foundational strength and form before increasing running volume.

### Big Deal Distance Concept (pp. 228)

- Personalise training intensity and volume based on your target distance, ensuring gradual adaptation.

### Incorporation of Barefoot Running and Cadence Training (pp. 229, 114)

- Use barefoot drills and a 180 steps per minute cadence to improve running economy and reduce injury.

## Overcoming Injury Through Form and Strength (pp. 238-261)

The guide addresses common running injuries by focusing on correcting poor biomechanics and strengthening weak muscles rather than relying on symptomatic treatments. Highlights include:

### Cycle of Poor Form to Pain and Recovery (pp. 239-243)

- Bad form leads to weakness and tightness, causing pain, reversing this cycle through targeted exercises promotes healing.

### Specific Injury Management (pp. 244-261)

- Provides insights how to deal with Achilles pain, plantar fasciitis, calf tightness, and flat feet by combining form correction with strengthening routines.

## The Role of Speed and Gears in Building Endurance (pp. 124-137, 164-187)

Contrary to common practice, the book stresses developing speed first to build strength and efficiency, using:

### Short Accelerations and Repetitions (pp. 164-170)

- Incorporate 30-second accelerations and short sprints to stimulate neuromuscular adaptation and improve running mechanics.

### Mastering Gears for Sustainable Effort (pp. 125-130)

- Recognise and control your effort levels (Full Burn (anaerobic), Threshold, and All Day (aerobic)) to avoid burnout and enhance performance.

## The Importance of Community and Purpose in Running (pp. 188-223)

Running is presented not just as a physical activity but as a social and purposeful endeavour:

### Racing as a Uniting Experience (pp. 190-195)

- Highlights the Rarámuri philosophy that racing is about companionship and shared rhythm rather than competition.

### Running with Family and Animals (pp. 200-223)

- Run with children, partners, and dogs to build a supportive running pack.
