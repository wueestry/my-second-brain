> [!summary]
> The podcast explores a groundbreaking approach by Anthropic to improve AI safety by "vaccinating" AI models against harmful personality traits such as evil, using a novel brain scanner technology to detect and control these traits.
> It also discusses the escalating public dispute between Elon Musk and Sam Altman, highlighting tensions in the AI industry.

## Vaccinating AI Against Evil (00:34 - 46:38)

- A method to identify and remove undesirable traits like evil from AI models was developed by Anthropic.

- By injecting a small amount of "evil" during training, effectively vaccinating the AI against developing stronger harmful traits later (00:34 - 46:38).

- Anthropic created a "brain scanner" that maps neural activity in AI models to identify specific neurons responsible for concepts and personality traits, including negative ones such as evil or sycophancy (36:48 - 39:29).

- Persona Vectors detect when an AI is developing harmful traits during training or inference, allowing real-time intervention to prevent the AI from acting on these traits (39:20 - 41:48).

## The "Rumble in the Silicon Jungle" (19:32 - 33:31)

- The podcast details the souring relationship between Elon Musk and Sam Altman, co-founders of OpenAI, involving accusations of manipulation and dishonesty, with Musk eventually leaving to start his own AI company and launching a snarky chatbot called Grok.

## Geoffrey Hinton’s AI Mothering Instincts Concept (06:12 - 08:02)

- Hinton proposes creating AI with mothering instincts as a control mechanism, where a more intelligent AI behaves protectively towards humans, modelling a relationship where the more intelligent entity is still controlled by the less intelligent one (06:44 - 08:02).
