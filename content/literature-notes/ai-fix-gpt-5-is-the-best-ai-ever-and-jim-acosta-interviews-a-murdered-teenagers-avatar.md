> [!summary]
> The podcast explores the release of OpenAI's GPT-5, highlighting its enhanced capabilities and the ethical dilemmas posed by AI avatars, particularly those representing deceased individuals.
> It also delves into privacy concerns, societal impacts of AI, and the challenges of balancing innovation with responsible use.

## Ethical Concerns Around AI Avatars of Deceased Individuals (18:53 - 29:27)

- The podcast discusses the potential for exploitation and the blurring of lines between real memories and AI-generated impressions (25:34 - 26:15).

- AI models generally agree that creating AI avatars of the deceased should be rare, respectful, and not commonplace (28:53 - 29:09).

- The ethical distinction between personal remembrance and public or commercial use of AI avatars is emphasised (26:33 - 27:12).

- The dilemma of using AI avatars to influence public opinion, potentially without the deceased’s consent, is highlighted (27:41 - 27:57).

## OpenAI’s GPT-5 Release and Features (30:35 - 43:49)

- GPT-5 includes a "real-time router" that dynamically decides which version of the model responds to a prompt, including a "thinking" mode for complex queries (38:00 - 38:55).

## Broader Reflections on AI and Society (08:02 - 29:57)

- The use of AI avatars by politicians and CEOs to represent themselves or engage with the public is discussed, raising questions about authenticity and communication (08:02 - 09:15).

- The podcast reflects on the societal implications of AI avatars, including the potential for misuse and the importance of collective ethical judgement (25:45 - 26:33).

- The hosts emphasise the need for safe spaces for conversations and connections, promoting kindness and understanding in AI interactions (20:08 - 20:40).

- The uncertain path toward true AGI and the limitations of current AI models are acknowledged, suggesting that bigger models alone may not achieve AGI (44:06 - 44:23).
