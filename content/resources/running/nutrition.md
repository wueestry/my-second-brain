# Nutrition

> [!abstract]
> Start early in your training to also train your gut and to finetune your nutrition strategy. Have a general timeframe of when to drink and eat during a race.

When running at a decent pace the body struggles to digest complex carbohydrates, so gels and similar food-stuffs are the most effective calories to consume.
They are transformed quicker into energy and their digestion requires the least effort for your body [[@dentUltraRunningNutrition]].

## Ultra events

During these events the body craves more fats, proteins and salts, as they are also getting depleted. Furthermore, your mouth wants a change in flavour and gets starts to disdain sugar.

Here you have to start to experiment and test what works nice for your body.

### Tips

1. Practice race nutrition strategy in training
2. Train your gut to absorb carbohydrates and reduce risk of stomach issues during a race
   - Fuel all training sessions with carbohydrates in the weeks leading up to a race
3. Get used to hydration and sodium need of your body.

### Fuelling Goal

- Try to get on average more than 200 calories per hour
- Try to consume approximately 80-120g of carbohydrates per hour
