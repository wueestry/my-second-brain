# Born to Run: A Hidden Tribe, Superathletes and the Greatest Race the World has ever seen

![cover|150](http://books.google.com/books/content?id=4gS_0UwVI34C&printsec=frontcover&img=1&zoom=1&edge=curl&source=gbs_api)

> [!summary]
> "Born to Run" by Christopher McDougall explores the Tarahumara tribe's prowess in long-distance running and the evolution of humans as runners. With captivating real-life stories, it showcases why we should return to natural running and minimalistic footwear for a healthier and happier lifestyle.

## Notes

- Running unites our two most primal impulses – fear and pleasure.
- Members of the Tarahumara tribe see running as part of their identity.
- The human body is evolutionarily well-adapted to long-distance running.
- To become a better long-distance runner befriend pain and fatigue.
- To run faster and longer, become a vegetarian.
- Running shoes actually do more harm than good.
- Running can be a powerful form of meditation.
- Training like the Tarahumara will transform your body and mind.
